Student Robotics Newbie Guidelines

For New SR Members

Don’t run away just yet, we’re really nice people (and we’ll give you cake).

  1. Do ask if you need help, although it might be worth having a quick google first for the information. Our wiki contains an awful lot of information.
  2. If you’ve never used a revision control system before (or don’t even know what that means) please ask someone about git, we use it to store nearly everything we do.
  3. Don’t be scared to ask things.
  4. Fill the list with all your SR related thoughts, we love information.
  5. Don’t be afraid of looking silly. We’ve all done it within SR at one point.

For Old SR Members

Don’t bite the newbies, these people think we’re doing cool stuff, let’s not scare them away just yet.

  1. Don’t use specialised terms unless you’re sure the Newbie is familiar with the term. Examples are ‘BOM’, ‘Gerbers’, ‘Geda’ and ‘PWM’ (depending on what background they come from). Do: ask them if they know what a ‘BOM’ is and then if they don’t tell them.
  2. Don’t assume they know what they’re doing, especially if they say that they know what they’re doing. Do: check on their progress, help them get their progress to others using git.
  3. Don’t mercilessly critique their work in the same way you would the IDE, something Rob or Rich has made or jump in and change it for them. Do: try to be constructive in criticism, review things with them at the same time that they’re doing them.
  4. Don’t give them a task and then leave them on their own to do it. Do: walk through everything they need help with, face to face, for at least a few months. (yes Months).
  5. Don’t ask them to google it, link them to google, or send them to just fucking google it unless you’ve repeatedly beaten the thing that they’re trying to do a thousand times.
